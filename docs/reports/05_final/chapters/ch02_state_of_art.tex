\section{Port Scan Attack Detection}
\tobewritten{Anas El Kartouti --- define port scanning, taxonomy of scan types}

\subsection{Definition}
\tobewritten{Anas El Kartouti}

A port scan is a technique used to discover open ports and services on a target host. Attackers use port scanning as a reconnaissance phase to map the attack surface before launching exploits. Common scan types include SYN scan, TCP Connect scan, UDP scan, XMAS scan, and NULL scan.

\subsection{Taxonomy of Port Scan Techniques}
\tobewritten{Anas El Kartouti --- table of scan types with descriptions}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{Classification of port scan techniques}
\label{tab:scan_taxonomy}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{lX}
\toprule
\textbf{Scan Type} & \textbf{Description} \\
\midrule
SYN Scan & Half-open scan using SYN packets without completing TCP handshake \\
TCP Connect & Full TCP connection establishment to each port \\
UDP Scan & Sends UDP packets to detect open UDP services \\
XMAS Scan & Sets FIN, PSH, URG flags to elicit responses from closed ports \\
ACK Scan & Maps firewall rules by analyzing RST responses \\
Service Detection & Probes identified open ports for service version information \\
OS Detection & Analyzes TCP/IP stack behavior to fingerprint the operating system \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}
